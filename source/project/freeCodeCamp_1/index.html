Clair Cameron Patterson

The man who saved us all from Saturnus.
Dr. Clair Cameron Patterson.

"I don’t have any pride, I’m sorry to say. I have zero pride in any award. All I feel is obligation, obligation, and obligation." -- Clair Cameron Patterson

    Here's a time line of Clair Cameron Patterson life:

  • 1922 - born in Mitchellville, Iowa
  • 1940 - first suggests contamination of Lead at the University of Chicago
  • 1944 - work on nuclear production facility in Oak Ridge, Tennessee"
  • 1946 - returns as a chemistry Ph.D. student at the University of Chicago
  • 1951 - received his Ph.D by confirming the age of a billion-year-old hunk of granite
  • 1953 - with same method, he determines the Earth was 4.5 billion years old.
  • 1965 - tries to draw public attention to the problem of increased lead levels in the environment and the food chain from lead from industrial sourcey. However, His contracts is refused by many research organizations including Public Health Service
  • 1971 - being excluded from a National Research Council (NRC) panel on atmospheric lead contamination, even though he is then the foremost expert on the subject.
  • 1975 - The United States mandated the use of unleaded gasoline to protect catalytic converters in all new cars. This process is accelerated by Patterson's efforts
  • 1978 - publish a 78-page minority report in National Research Council panel argued that control measures should start immediately, including gasoline, food containers, paint, glazes, and water distribution systems, but other members argued the need for more research before giving recommending action
  • 1990 - lead levels within the blood of Americans are reported to have dropped by up to 80%
  • 1995 - dies at the age of 73

“Today, scientists sound the alarm on other environmental dangers. Vested interests still hire their own scientists to confuse the issue. But in the end, nature will not be fooled”--- Neil Tyson
